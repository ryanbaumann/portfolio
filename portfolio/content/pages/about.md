I lead Developer Experience Engineering for Google Maps Platform. I build systems that turn recurring developer friction into shipped tools, clearer paths, and measurable adoption.

My team works across product, engineering, UX, technical writing, and field teams. Recent work includes [Code Assist](https://developers.google.com/maps/ai/code-assist), [agent skills](https://github.com/googlemaps/agent-skills), agentic evals, the [Geo Architecture Center](https://developers.google.com/maps/architecture), and open-source client libraries.

I stay close to the work by reviewing traces, writing evals, dogfooding products, and shipping reference apps. The loop is simple: find a problem that repeats, build the smallest useful artifact, test whether it helps, and publish what others can reuse. I explain the operating model in [DevX Is a Growth Function](/writing/devx-is-a-growth-discipline/).

Before this role, I led product and engineering for [Intelligent Product Essentials](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/data-analytics/introducing-intelligent-products-essentials-for-manufacturers) at Google Cloud. At Instabase, I built the solutions architecture team. At Mapbox, I joined as the first customer-facing engineer, later led solutions architecture, helped launch Boundaries and Atlas, and wrote open-source tools for Python notebooks and Power BI. Earlier, I built connected-worksite systems at Caterpillar.

I studied mechanical engineering at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Before software, I raced professionally for Trek-Livestrong and Bahati Foundation. Racing taught me to pace long efforts, measure the right signal, and keep looking for the next useful improvement. I still chase trails.
